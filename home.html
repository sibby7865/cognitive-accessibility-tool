Introduction

Welcome to the Cognitive Accessibility Tool. This tool helps you find and fix barriers that make websites hard to use for people with cognitive differences. To use it, enter a URL and click Scan to generate a detailed report of potential issues.

You will receive clear recommendations written in plain language, with examples showing where each issue appears. Each recommendation explains why the issue matters and how to address it effectively. After making changes you can rescan your page to confirm that all critical issues are resolved.

Our goal is to help you create a more inclusive and accessible online experience for all users.

Purpose of the Tool

Many websites include design and content choices that unintentionally make reading and navigation harder for people with dyslexia, ADHD, or memory difficulties. This tool helps you identify those specific barriers on any webpage.

Most accessibility checkers focus on technical code compliance and often overlook how content feels to users with cognitive differences. This tool takes a different approach by combining targeted cognitive checks with a built-in Readability Checker that measures reading age and ease of understanding.

It provides clear, practical recommendations in plain English, with examples that show exactly what to change and why.

Some coding knowledge is helpful, but the tool explains things where needed so that writers, designers, and editors can all use it confidently. You can scan, revise, and repeat until your site is easier to read, navigate, and understand for everyone.

Key Features

  • Profile-Driven Checks: Select from General, Dyslexia, ADHD, or Memory profiles. Each scan runs checks tailored to that specific need, focusing only on what matters most for the chosen profile.
  • Instant, Live Scanning: Enter any URL and get results within seconds. There’s no setup, installation, or sign-up required.
  • Plain-English Recommendations: Receive clear explanations of what to fix and why, with examples to guide you.
  • Integrated Readability Checker: View UK reading-age and reading-ease scores, with colour-coded paragraph-level feedback to help you spot content that may be too complex.

How This Tool Is Different to Existing Tools?

Most accessibility tools such as Google Lighthouse or axe-core are built around code-level checks (missing ARIA attributes, semantic HTML errors, color-contrast ratios). They tell you what breaks a guideline but say little about how your content actually feels to someone with dyslexia, ADHD, or memory challenges.

Our scanner takes a user-centered approach. You begin by selecting a profile: General, Dyslexia, ADHD, or Memory and the tool runs only the checks most relevant to that group. This keeps your report focused and fast, rather than overwhelming you with hundreds of unrelated issues.

Every finding comes with a plain-English explanation and a concrete example of where to make the change. You won’t see pages of technical jargon, just clear guidance on what to fix and why it matters, backed by a snippet from your own page.

We also include a Readability Checker powered by the Flesch–Kincaid formulas. You receive both a UK reading-age score and a 0–100 reading-ease score, with colour-coded feedback at the paragraph level. This lets you spot dense or confusing passages.

No complex setup, no custom scripts, and no lengthy batch reports. Scan any URL in seconds, apply the practical recommendations, then re-scan immediately to confirm your improvements. This rapid iteration workflow helps you keep accessibility at the forefront of your design process.

How the Scan Works

1. Select a Profile: Choose General, Dyslexia, ADHD, or Memory to tailor the checks for your audience.

2. Enter a URL and Click Scan: The tool crawls your page and evaluates only the selected checks in seconds.

3. Review Your Results: Each row shows the issue name, count, recommendation, issue location snippet, and guidance for interpreting that example.

4. Implement Fixes and Re-scan: Update your content or code, then run another scan to confirm all critical issues have been resolved.

ADHD: Flags autoplay media, flashing content, and dense text to reduce distractions and cognitive overload.

Memory Difficulties: Ensures your site has logical, consistent headings; clear, easy-to-use navigation; and summary or overview sections so users can quickly remember and return to key information.

Readability Checker

Even well-designed pages can include dense or complex text. Our Readability Checker evaluates two key metrics:

  • UK Reading Age: The school year or age range at which most readers can understand the text.
  • Reading Ease: A score from 0–100. Higher scores mean the text is easier to read and understand.

Each paragraph is highlighted in green (easy), amber (moderate), or red (difficult) so you can spot problem areas at a glance. Use the “Show Problems Only” filter to focus on paragraphs below your target threshold.

Getting Started

  1. Run a scan: Go to Accessibility Evaluation, choose a profile, enter your page’s URL, and click Scan.
  2. Review your issues: Look at each flagged problem, note how many times it occurs, and read the plain-English recommendation.
  3. Apply fixes: Update your site content or code based on those suggestions, then scan again until no critical issues remain.
  4. Check readability: Switch to Readability Checker, enter the same URL, and review your UK reading-age and ease scores.
